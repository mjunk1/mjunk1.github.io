Research Interests

My research interests lie in the classical simulation of quantum circuits and the characterisation of quantum systems. To this end, I am developping advanced methods based on the stabiliser formalism and the Clifford group.

In particular, I am interested in stabiliser-based simulation methods such as Wigner functions on discrete phase space and decompositions in stabiliser frames (e.g. robustness of magic, stabiliser extent, dyadic negativity). I have worked on the closely related resource theory of magic and am interested in resource-theoretic questions in quantum computing.

Moroever, I am studying unitary t-designs and their applications to protocols in quantum system identification. Among others, I am interested in randomised benchmarking, RB tomography and gate set tomography.
